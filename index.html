The Meta problem

Meta works for real estate when the account is built to qualify demand, not just collect leads.

From what I see inside broker, agency, and developer accounts, more than 80% of teams use Meta as a basic form-filling tool instead of a buyer acquisition system: they show the same generic property message to everyone, ask too little in the form, follow up too slowly, and then decide the platform is the problem when the sales team hears, "I was just looking."

The real issue is usually much deeper than the campaign setup; you need to understand how Meta learns, which signals it can and cannot read in real estate, how housing restrictions change targeting, why the form has to qualify intent before the call, and how the creative has to speak to the buyer's situation before the click even happens.

With the right approach, you can generate qualified leads cheaper than many portal-driven acquisition channels and close more real estate deals. My results and my clients' results prove it across markets where buyers also use portals like Property Finder, Bayut, Idealista, Zillow, Rightmove, and Otodom.

That is why I created this guide: to help you start generating qualified leads within a week and increase your income from real estate transactions with a system you actually understand.

The myth

Your buyers are already on Meta, but your offer has to be strong enough to stop the scroll.

Many brokers, real estate agencies, and developers are convinced that Meta cannot sell $200,000, $500,000, or $1,000,000+ property. Or they assume it is simply too expensive and too unstable to be a serious acquisition channel.

That is a myth. Your clients - families buying homes, investors protecting capital, founders relocating, people looking for second homes - have phones. They use Instagram and Facebook. In 2026, it is almost impossible to imagine a serious buyer with no social media footprint.

The job of your advertising is not to close a property deal inside the feed. The job is to interrupt the right person at the right moment: while they are scrolling before work, reading news, watching Reels, or liking a photo from someone they know. Your ad has to create interest and move them into a qualified conversation.

Inside the guide, I show you how to find those buyers, make them care, qualify them before your sales team wastes time, and make them choose you when they are ready to buy or rent. This is how Meta becomes another stable source of income for your real estate business.
