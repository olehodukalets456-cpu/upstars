The real problem

Most real estate Meta campaigns do not die in Ads Manager.

They die after the form.

The ad gets the click. The form gets the lead. Then the lead gets a generic message, a PDF catalog, or a slow follow-up from someone who has no idea why that person clicked in the first place.

That is where the money leaks. Not in the button you picked. Not in some hidden targeting setting. Not because “Meta leads are bad.”

You are asking Meta for buyers, but feeding it weak signals.

  • Generic creative
  • Mixed audiences
  • Soft forms
  • No lead quality feedback
  • No real follow-up protocol
  • No clean decision rule for when to scale or kill
What I do

I run Meta ads for real estate across markets, price points, and buyer types.

Investors. Families. Relocation buyers. Capital preservers. Second-home buyers. High-ticket villa buyers. Off-plan buyers. People who are ready now and people who are “just looking” but will move in six months.
